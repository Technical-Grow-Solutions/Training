Vapor Pressure Deficit.

Literal translation Vapor = vapour, Pressure = pressure, Deficit = difference.
Or the difference in vapor pressure.

Science-minded growers prefer VPD as it provides some useful absolute data in the ever-changing world of air temperature moisture holding capacity.
Temperature and relative humidity actually affect your plants.
VPD is an advanced grow room metric that combines air temperature, leaf temperature and relative humidity into a simple unit of pressure. (KPa)
When you understand VPD you are on your way to a successful grower.

Hold the infrared thermometer on the leaf of the plant for a few minutes and a temperature measurement is taken.
Through the measurement, the controller adjusts the relative humidity of the grow cabinet.

It is good to have a temperature difference of 2C between leaf and air temperature, if not, the lamp may be hanging too low.


3 concepts:
  • VPSat - Vapor pressure (evaporation pressure) in the leaf of your plant.
  • VPAir - Saturated vapor pressure of your grow room.
  • VPD - The difference between VPSat and VPAir.
    •


    To calculate the correct VPD values you need 3 values:

  • TAir - Room temperature.
  • R - Relative humidity of the room.
  • TLeaf - Temperature of the leaf.
    •

    De formules:
    VPSat = 610.7 * 10((7.5 * TLeaf)/(237.3 + TLeaf)) / 1000
    VPAir = 610.7 * 10((7.5 * TAir)/(237.3 + TAir)) * (R / 100) / 1000

    VPD = VPSat - VPAir

    A low VPD value:

  • Relative humidity of the room is high.
  • Lower evaporation effect, less perspiration.
  • Good for Propagation.
  • Too low can lead to mold or other plant diseases, but also to poor nutrient absorption, especially calcium.

    • A high VPD value:
  • Relative humidity of the room is low.
  • Greater evaporation effect, greater perspiration.
  • Well at the Bloom.
