Beneath the top layer of the growing plant, the roots are busy at the same time keeping the plant stable and absorbing substances that nourish the plant.
The root area consists of the main root, which all branches out making it a fibrous mass.
The roots are part of the vascular system of the plant also called the xylem.

Cotyledons are embryonic leaves that occur in seed-bearing plants.
They form during the development of the embryo, along with the meristem of the root and stem.

Cotyledons are the very first leaflets that appear after germination.
The green color of the cotyledons indicates the presence of chlorophyll, which makes photosynthesis possible in the earliest phase of growth.
In fact, the lobes are small solar panels that get the plant working.
Shortly after, the first pair of true leaves appear and the plant sheds the lobe leaves, as they are no longer needed.

The main stem arises from the roots and gives the plant the support to grow straight up and is a barrel highway,
which makes the water and minerals go up from the roots.

It is also integrated with the phloem.
Secondary growth arises from the main stem and has a leaf node.

The leaves emerge in pairs from a leaf node on each side of the stem.
A node (new branch) arises in the axil of the trunk and the stem of the leaf.

The distance between 2 different nodes is called the internode.
Indica - In dominant variants, the internode distance is shorter..
Sativa - In dominant variants, the internode distance is greater.

There are 3 difference seeds you can buy.

Regulair - the outcome can be feminesed or masculair..
Autoflower - the plant goes self in flowering stage..
Feminised - the outcome is always 99,9% feminised.

There are 4 different species of cannabis.

Sativa, Indica and Ruderalis
Hybrides are a mix of 2 or more (also with the Autoflower!) varieties.

Indica

Dark deep green, short, and broad leaves, usually with 7 to 9 leaf fingers.
It is believed that the increased amount of chlorophyll speeds up the flowering period of indica strains.

It is believed that the reduced amount of chlorophyll is partly responsible for the longer flowering period.

Sativa

Light, lime-green leaves with thin long, up to 13 leaf fingers.

Ruderalis

Fairly narrow leaves with only three to five slender leaf fingers.
The leaves have evolved to give autoflower the ability to flower on its own regardless of the number of hours of light received.

Fan leaves(Fan leaves) also have the same symmetry and also grow in pairs from the main stem and branches.
You can also tell the difference between an indica and a sativa marijuana by the leaves.

The leaves are essential for the photosynthesis and should not be pruned excessively, which can be removed in the shade in the late flowering stage, otherwise they keeping the lower tops from the light.

Sugar leaves (Sugar leaves) are usually all the leaves in the top of which you clearly cannot see the stem.
They often contain as much trichomes (THC) as the bud itself.

With a microscope you can see that it looks like they have been sprinkled with fine granulated sugar.
Keep the sugar leaves full of trichomes that make your cutting waste valuable, you can still make anything out of it.
Please note that for some applications it is best to freeze the sugar leaves immediately after harvesting.

In the leaves, the assimilates are produced by photosynthesis under the influence of light and transported to all other parts of the plant via the phloem.

Assimilation is a biochemical process in which an organic compound is built from simpler organic compounds or from inorganic substances, especially carbon dioxide and < B>water.
This requires energy in the form of light.

Phloem is a type of tissue in plants and ensures the transport of building materials in the plant from the leaves to the growing and storage parts of the plant.

Xylem, on the other hand, is responsible for the transport of water and the nutrients dissolved in it, only in one direction, from roots to the leaves ('up').
Both tissues consist of different types of cells: sieve vessels or basal vessels, sieve cells and companion cells.

Topping is simply an all-encompassing term for the flowers that come out

If you see a 'white hair'(pistil) popping up in a leaf node before the flowering stage, it means it is a female plant.

The males plant does not produce buds when in flower, but stamens with anthers that are full of pollen a few weeks earlier than the females.
Nor will it necessarily be the largest and most powerful plant in the garden.

It is always good to wait a while before drawing conclusions until the flowers can really be observed.
If the pistil is not present and a cluster of grapes begins to appear, then you know for sure that it is a male.

Buds are not just a scattered clump of leaves and sticky resin.
Calyxes are the small clusters that resemble a teardrop that hold the cannabis flower together.

Sugar leaves grow between and around the calyxes.
The hard-to-find calyx won't show until later in the flowering period as the buds begin to swell.

Only the unpollinated flowers bloom in abundance.
If the pistil is pollinated, the calyxes act as ovaries and the task changes to seed incubator.

Some buds can become very hard with compact clusters of many small calyxes, others have larger calyxes in a slightly looser formation.
In general, it is the Indica influence that gives the cannabis the cleanest look.

Pistils are what people sometimes call the 'hairs' or 'nuggs'.
Together with the calyxes, which contain eggs, these are the plant's reproductive organs.

The pistils are the outgrowth of the calyxes and are there to collect pollen, the female wants to be fertilized and as long as she is ignored, flower production will be her focus.
Before your plant goes into flower, the female has a separate pistil from a leaf node and helps identify your female plants.

During the flowering period they will have different colors from white to orange/red as the buds mature.

A colaB> is simply the flowering top of the female plant.

Of course, different growing methods and techniques will give you multiple buds.(Buds)
But it is the tops of the stem that are called colas.
